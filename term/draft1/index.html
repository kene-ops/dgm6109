Relationship Between Workout, Sleep, Feeding and Productivity

Kenechukwu Okongwu

Dataset Description

My dataset consists of daily personal observations collected between January and March 2026. Each daily record includes measurements of sleep duration, meditation duration, number of meals, gym/workout level, and productivity level. The goal of collecting this data is to ascertain how my gym/workout level affect my level of productivity. It further assesses the influence of other listed daily activities on my daily productivity.

Each observation is recorded as a single day, allowing analysis of correlations between gym, sleep, meditation, feeding and productivity level.

  • Date: The day the observation was recorded.
  • Gym Activity: Number of workout repetitions performed that day.
  • Meals: Number of meals consumed that day.
  • Meditation: Minutes spent meditating.
  • Sleep Duration: Categorized as low, medium, or high.
  • Productivity: Self-assessed productivity score ranging from 1 to 5.

Hypothesis

As my workout increases, my productivity increases.

Data Elements Used

  • Gym (number of reps)
  • Productivity (1 - 5)
  • Sleep Duration (minutes)
  • Meals (count/day)

Visualization Design

The visualization uses a scatterplot format to represent relationships between the variables (gym, sleep, feeding and productivity). Each rectangle represents one day of observations. Multiple visual encodings are used to display different aspects of the dataset simultaneously. in addition to gym level, the visualization further assessed the impact of improved feeding and sleeping duration on productivity. This project examines how these lifestyle variables interact and how they relate to my daily productivity levels.

  • X-Axis: Gym activity (number of workout repetitions).
  • Y-Axis: Productivity level.
  • Rectangle: Represents an individual observation.
  • Color: Represents sleep duration.
  • Dots inside rectangles: Represent number of meals eaten that day.

Color Legend

  • Yellow – Low sleep duration
  • Orange – Medium sleep duration
  • Green – High sleep duration

Dots Legend

  • (.) - One meal
  • (..) - Two meals
  • (...) - Three meals

Interpretation

Initial observations suggest that higher workout activity tends to appear alongside higher productivity levels. Days with sufficient sleep and regular meals also appear to correspond with stronger productivity outcomes. However, some exceptions exist in the dataset, indicating that productivity is influenced by multiple factors and not solely by exercise or sleep.

Each rectangle plotted in the chart represents a daily observation from the dataset. The visualization allows viewers to compare workout levels, sleep patterns, feeding habits, and productivity simultaneously.
